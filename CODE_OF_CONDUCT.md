# Code of Conduct

> **Strong Roots. Inspired Growth.**

BAOBAB is built on the belief that exceptional software is created by people who collaborate with professionalism, integrity, respect, and a shared commitment to excellence.

This Code of Conduct establishes the standards of behaviour expected from everyone who participates in the BAOBAB community. It reflects our commitment to creating a welcoming, respectful, and collaborative environment where contributors from diverse backgrounds can work together to build secure, reliable, and sustainable software.

---

# Purpose

The purpose of this Code of Conduct is to:

- Foster a professional, respectful, and inclusive community.
- Encourage constructive collaboration and knowledge sharing.
- Promote integrity in all community interactions.
- Provide clear expectations for behaviour.
- Establish a fair and transparent process for addressing concerns.
- Support the long-term sustainability of the BAOBAB community.

This document applies to all contributors, maintainers, reviewers, users, and anyone participating in BAOBAB community activities.

This document should be read together with:

- `README.md`
- `CONTRIBUTING.md`
- `SECURITY.md`
- `CHANGELOG.md`

---

# Our Commitment

The BAOBAB community is committed to providing an environment where everyone can participate with confidence, dignity, and mutual respect.

We welcome contributors regardless of their experience level, professional background, geographic location, or area of expertise.

Every participant should feel able to:

- Ask questions.
- Share ideas.
- Offer constructive feedback.
- Learn from others.
- Contribute without fear of harassment or personal attacks.
- Participate in technical discussions respectfully.

Our shared objective is to build outstanding software while cultivating an engineering community characterised by professionalism, collaboration, and continuous learning.

---

# Community Values

The following values guide every interaction within the BAOBAB community.

## Respect

Treat every person with courtesy, patience, and consideration.

Respect differing perspectives, recognise that reasonable people may reach different technical conclusions, and engage in discussions with an open mind.

---

## Professionalism

Conduct yourself in a manner that reflects positively on both the project and its community.

Communicate clearly, act responsibly, honour commitments where possible, and approach collaboration with maturity and integrity.

---

## Integrity

Be honest in your communications, acknowledge mistakes, give credit where it is due, and represent your work accurately.

Integrity builds trust, and trust is essential to successful collaboration.

---

## Collaboration

Great software is rarely built in isolation.

Share knowledge, support fellow contributors, and recognise that collaboration often produces stronger outcomes than individual effort.

Constructive teamwork is encouraged across all aspects of the project.

---

## Excellence

Strive for quality in both technical work and community interactions.

We value thoughtful engineering, continuous improvement, careful review, and a willingness to learn from experience.

---

## Accountability

Take responsibility for your actions and contributions.

Be willing to receive constructive feedback, correct mistakes, and help maintain a healthy and productive community.

---

# Guiding Principles

The BAOBAB community is guided by the following principles.

## Assume Positive Intent

Approach discussions with the expectation that others are acting in good faith unless there is clear evidence to the contrary.

Misunderstandings are often resolved through respectful communication.

---

## Focus on Ideas

Technical discussions should focus on the quality of ideas, designs, and implementations—not on individuals.

Disagreement is both natural and valuable when expressed respectfully and supported by sound reasoning.

---

## Learn Continuously

Every contributor has something to teach and something to learn.

We encourage curiosity, mentoring, knowledge sharing, and continuous professional growth.

---

## Welcome Constructive Feedback

Thoughtful feedback strengthens both contributors and the project.

Offer feedback respectfully, receive it graciously, and use it as an opportunity for improvement.

---

## Value Diversity of Experience

BAOBAB benefits from contributors with different skills, experiences, disciplines, and perspectives.

Diverse viewpoints contribute to stronger engineering decisions and more resilient software.

---

## Build Trust

Trust is earned through honesty, consistency, transparency, and respect.

Every interaction should strengthen confidence within the community and reinforce our shared commitment to the project's long-term success.

---

# Living the Code

A Code of Conduct is more than a document—it is reflected in the everyday actions of the community.

Every conversation, code review, design discussion, bug report, documentation update, and Pull Request is an opportunity to demonstrate the values described here.

By participating in BAOBAB, each community member contributes not only to the platform itself but also to the culture that sustains it.

# Expected Behaviour

Every participant is expected to contribute to a professional, respectful, and collaborative community.

Examples of expected behaviour include:

- Treating everyone with dignity, courtesy, and respect.
- Listening actively and considering alternative viewpoints.
- Providing constructive and evidence-based feedback.
- Communicating honestly and professionally.
- Being patient with contributors of varying experience levels.
- Sharing knowledge generously.
- Taking responsibility for mistakes and learning from them.
- Respecting community decisions once they have been reached.
- Helping maintain a welcoming and productive environment.

Professional conduct strengthens both the community and the software we build together.

---

# Professional Communication

Clear and respectful communication is essential to effective collaboration.

Community members are encouraged to:

- Write clearly and respectfully.
- Keep discussions focused on the topic.
- Explain technical reasoning where appropriate.
- Ask questions rather than making assumptions.
- Seek clarification before drawing conclusions.
- Provide feedback that is specific, constructive, and actionable.
- Recognise and acknowledge valuable contributions made by others.

Written communication should reflect the same professionalism expected in face-to-face collaboration.

---

# Constructive Technical Disagreement

Engineering projects benefit from thoughtful technical debate.

Contributors are encouraged to:

- Challenge ideas respectfully.
- Support opinions with evidence, experience, or technical reasoning.
- Welcome alternative solutions.
- Remain open to changing their position when presented with better evidence.
- Separate technical issues from personal opinions.
- Accept project decisions once discussions have concluded.

Disagreement should improve decisions—not damage relationships.

---

# Inclusive Collaboration

BAOBAB welcomes contributors from a wide range of backgrounds, experiences, disciplines, and skill levels.

To support an inclusive community, contributors should:

- Welcome newcomers.
- Encourage participation.
- Be patient when answering questions.
- Avoid unnecessary jargon where clearer language is available.
- Recognise that contributors may have different levels of experience.
- Value diverse perspectives and approaches to problem solving.

An inclusive engineering community produces stronger ideas and more resilient software.

---

# Mentorship and Knowledge Sharing

Learning is an important part of every engineering community.

Experienced contributors are encouraged to:

- Share knowledge openly.
- Explain decisions where practical.
- Support new contributors.
- Encourage continuous learning.
- Provide constructive guidance during reviews.
- Help others develop technical confidence.

Strong communities invest in the growth of their members.

---

# Unacceptable Behaviour

The following behaviours are inconsistent with BAOBAB's values and will not be tolerated.

Examples include:

## Harassment

Including:

- Intimidation.
- Threats.
- Bullying.
- Persistent unwanted contact.
- Deliberate attempts to exclude others.

---

## Discrimination

Discriminatory behaviour directed toward any individual or group is unacceptable.

Every participant deserves equal respect and fair treatment within the community.

---

## Personal Attacks

Criticising people rather than discussing ideas, code, or technical decisions undermines productive collaboration.

Focus discussions on the work—not the individual.

---

## Abusive or Offensive Conduct

Examples include:

- Insults.
- Hostile language.
- Deliberately inflammatory comments.
- Profanity directed at others.
- Humiliating or degrading behaviour.

Professional communication should always be maintained.

---

## Harassment Through Technology

Unacceptable behaviour also includes inappropriate conduct using:

- GitHub Issues.
- Pull Requests.
- Code review comments.
- Discussions.
- Email.
- Chat platforms.
- Social media where representing or engaging with the BAOBAB community.

Online interactions should meet the same standards expected in any professional setting.

---

## Deliberate Disruption

Examples include:

- Repeatedly derailing discussions.
- Intentionally obstructing collaboration.
- Knowingly submitting malicious code.
- Spamming community channels.
- Repeatedly ignoring established community processes.

Constructive disagreement is welcome; deliberate disruption is not.

---

## Misrepresentation

Community members should not:

- Misrepresent another person's work.
- Claim credit for contributions they did not make.
- Deliberately provide misleading technical information.
- Knowingly conceal significant project risks.
- Misrepresent the project's position or decisions.

Honesty and transparency are essential to maintaining trust.

---

# Respecting Intellectual Property

Contributors are expected to respect intellectual property rights.

Examples include:

- Providing appropriate attribution.
- Respecting software licences.
- Avoiding plagiarism.
- Contributing only material they have the right to share.
- Following applicable open-source licensing requirements.

Integrity extends to the responsible use of intellectual property.

---

# Confidential Information

Contributors may occasionally encounter confidential or sensitive information.

Such information should:

- Be treated responsibly.
- Be shared only with authorised individuals.
- Never be disclosed publicly without appropriate permission.
- Be handled in accordance with the project's security policies.

Protecting confidential information helps preserve trust in the project.

---

# Supporting a Healthy Community

Every community member can contribute positively by:

- Welcoming new contributors.
- Encouraging respectful discussion.
- Helping resolve misunderstandings.
- Redirecting conversations back to technical topics where necessary.
- Modelling the behaviour described in this Code of Conduct.

Healthy communities are built through many small, consistent actions.

---

---

# Reporting Concerns

Maintaining a respectful and professional community is a shared responsibility.

If you experience or witness behaviour that may violate this Code of Conduct, we encourage you to report it promptly.

Reports help protect both individuals and the community and allow concerns to be addressed fairly and consistently.

Where appropriate, reports should include:

- A clear description of the incident.
- Date and time.
- Relevant location (GitHub, email, discussion, meeting, etc.).
- Individuals involved.
- Supporting evidence, where available.
- Any immediate concerns regarding safety or confidentiality.

Reports should be made in good faith.

Knowingly submitting false or malicious reports is itself inconsistent with this Code of Conduct.

---

# Reporting Channels

Community concerns may be reported privately through the project's designated community contact.

For matters involving security vulnerabilities, please use:

**security@nabhold.com**

Behavioural concerns relating to this Code of Conduct should be directed to the project maintainers through the appropriate private communication channels identified by the repository.

Sensitive reports should never be discussed publicly unless authorised as part of an agreed resolution process.

---

# Confidentiality

Every report will be handled with appropriate discretion.

Information relating to an investigation will be shared only with individuals who require it to conduct a fair and thorough review.

Absolute confidentiality cannot always be guaranteed where disclosure is necessary to:

- Protect individuals.
- Investigate allegations.
- Comply with legal obligations.
- Implement appropriate corrective actions.

The privacy and dignity of everyone involved should be respected throughout the process.

---

# Informal Resolution

Not every disagreement requires a formal investigation.

Where appropriate, community members are encouraged to resolve misunderstandings through respectful dialogue, clarification, or mediation.

Examples may include:

- Miscommunication.
- Unintentional wording.
- Minor disagreements during technical discussions.
- Isolated incidents where no ongoing harm exists.

Informal resolution should never be used where:

- Harassment has occurred.
- Discrimination is alleged.
- Threats have been made.
- Individuals feel unsafe.
- Serious or repeated misconduct is involved.

In such circumstances, formal reporting is encouraged.

---

# Investigation Process

When a formal report is received, project maintainers will seek to ensure that concerns are handled fairly, consistently, and objectively.

The general process is as follows:

```text
Concern Reported
        │
        ▼
Acknowledgement
        │
        ▼
Initial Assessment
        │
        ▼
Information Gathering
        │
        ▼
Review by Maintainers
        │
        ▼
Decision
        │
        ▼
Corrective Action
        │
        ▼
Follow-up
```

The exact process may vary depending on the nature and seriousness of the concern.

---

# Principles of Investigation

Investigations should be guided by the following principles:

## Fairness

Every individual should have the opportunity to be heard.

---

## Objectivity

Decisions should be based on evidence rather than assumptions.

---

## Consistency

Similar situations should receive similar consideration whenever practical.

---

## Confidentiality

Sensitive information should be handled responsibly.

---

## Timeliness

Concerns should be addressed as promptly as circumstances permit.

---

## Respect

All participants should be treated professionally throughout the process.

---

# Enforcement Guidelines

The purpose of enforcement is to protect the community and encourage positive behaviour—not to punish unnecessarily.

Responses should be proportionate to the seriousness, frequency, and impact of the behaviour.

Possible responses include:

## Informal Guidance

Used where clarification or coaching is sufficient.

Examples include:

- Friendly reminders.
- Clarification of expectations.
- Educational guidance.

---

## Formal Warning

A written warning explaining:

- The behaviour of concern.
- Expected improvements.
- Possible consequences of repeated behaviour.

---

## Temporary Restrictions

Depending on the circumstances, temporary restrictions may include:

- Limited participation in discussions.
- Temporary suspension from community activities.
- Temporary loss of contribution privileges.

---

## Permanent Removal

In exceptional circumstances involving serious or repeated misconduct, individuals may be removed from participation in the BAOBAB community.

Such action should be taken only after careful consideration of the available evidence.

---

# Appeals

Individuals who are subject to enforcement actions may request that the decision be reconsidered.

Appeals should:

- Be submitted respectfully.
- Explain the grounds for reconsideration.
- Include any relevant additional information.

Appeals will be reviewed as fairly and objectively as practical.

The outcome of an appeal may confirm, modify, or overturn the original decision.

---

# Protection Against Retaliation

BAOBAB will not tolerate retaliation against individuals who:

- Report concerns in good faith.
- Participate in investigations.
- Provide truthful information.
- Support efforts to maintain a respectful community.

Retaliation undermines trust and is itself inconsistent with this Code of Conduct.

---

# Continuous Improvement

Every concern provides an opportunity to strengthen the community.

Where appropriate, lessons learned from investigations may be used to improve:

- Community guidance.
- Documentation.
- Contributor onboarding.
- Review processes.
- Communication practices.
- Governance documentation.

The objective is always to build a stronger and healthier engineering community.
